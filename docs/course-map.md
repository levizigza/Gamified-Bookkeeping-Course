# Course Map — Ledger Quest

Maps the 4-week bookkeeping curriculum to app worlds, challenges, and learning outcomes.

---

## Overview

```
Week 1 → Daily Ledger        → Record transactions (June 2024)
Week 2 → Account Sorter      → Classify & trial balance
Week 3 → Reports Room        → P&L & Balance Sheet
Week 4 → Year-End Boss Fight → Adjusting entries & handoff
```

**Practice business:** Bright Path Consulting (solo consulting, June 2024)

---

## Week 1 — Daily Ledger

### Learning objectives

- Define bookkeeping and why it matters (organization, business health, decisions, taxes, banks, partners, legal)
- Record everyday transactions: meals, entertainment, vehicle, office, supplies, travel, equipment
- Apply the **consistency** principle
- Understand **double-entry bookkeeping** (every debit has a credit)
- Handle **sales tax** on applicable sales and purchases
- Complete practice: record all transactions from **June 1–30, 2024**

### World: Daily Ledger

| Module | Challenge type | Skill |
|--------|----------------|-------|
| Why bookkeeping? | Scenario + reflection | Motivation & habits |
| Transaction types | Sort & label | Expense vs revenue vs asset |
| Double-entry basics | Journal entry builder | Debit/credit pairs |
| Sales tax | Entry with tax lines | Tax payable / recoverable |
| June ledger sprint | Timed recording sim | Full month practice |

### Sample June 2024 transactions (themes)

- Client invoice + payment (with/without sales tax)
- Office supplies (Staples)
- Business lunch with client
- Vehicle fuel and parking
- Home office internet (partial business use)
- Laptop equipment purchase
- Conference travel (flight, hotel, meals)
- Entertainment (team event)
- Software subscription
- Bank fee

### Badges

- **First Entry** — Post first journal line
- **Balanced Books** — Day with zero entry errors
- **June Complete** — All June transactions recorded

### Unlock

Completing June ledger sprint unlocks **Account Sorter**.

---

## Week 2 — Account Sorter

### Learning objectives

- Distinguish spending/selling with **immediate funds** vs **no immediate funds** (AP/AR, credit)
- Build and interpret a **trial balance**
- Classify accounts: fixed assets, current assets, long-term liabilities, current liabilities, income/revenue, expenses, equity

### World: Account Sorter

| Module | Challenge type | Skill |
|--------|----------------|-------|
| Cash vs accrual feel | Story choices | When cash moves vs when obligation exists |
| Account categories | Drag-and-drop sorter | Chart of accounts literacy |
| Trial balance puzzle | Fix imbalances | Debits = credits |
| Mixed transactions | Multi-step classify | Real-world ambiguity |
| TB boss | Generate TB from June data | Synthesis |

### Account category reference

| Category | Examples |
|----------|----------|
| Fixed assets | Equipment, vehicles |
| Current assets | Cash, AR, inventory, prepaid |
| Long-term liabilities | Loans payable (>1 yr) |
| Current liabilities | AP, sales tax payable, credit card |
| Income / revenue | Consulting fees, product sales |
| Expenses | Meals, travel, supplies, depreciation expense |
| Equity | Owner contributions, retained earnings |

### Badges

- **Category Master** — 90%+ on classification challenges
- **Trial Balance Hero** — First balanced TB from user entries

### Unlock

Balanced trial balance unlocks **Reports Room**.

---

## Week 3 — Reports Room

### Learning objectives

- Trial balance compiles into **Profit & Loss** and **Balance Sheet**
- Read reports for decisions (profitability, cash position, obligations)
- Connect daily entries to statement lines

### World: Reports Room

| Module | Challenge type | Skill |
|--------|----------------|-------|
| TB → P&L | Map accounts | Revenue & expense roll-up |
| TB → Balance Sheet | Map accounts | Assets, liabilities, equity |
| Report reading | Scenario Q&A | Interpret totals & trends |
| Decision lab | What-if stories | Use data to choose actions |
| Statement boss | Build both reports from June TB | Full synthesis |

### Key insights taught

- P&L = performance over a **period**
- Balance Sheet = position at a **point in time**
- Net income flows to equity (retained earnings)
- Assets = Liabilities + Equity

### Badges

- **P&L Reader** — Correctly answer 5 interpretation questions
- **Balance Sheet Builder** — Accurate BS from trial balance

### Unlock

Statement boss completion unlocks **Year-End Boss Fight**.

---

## Week 4 — Year-End Boss Fight

### Learning objectives

- Common **adjusting journals**: depreciation, home office, mileage
- Why year-end entries matter before tax prep
- Prepare a **handover package** for an accountant

### World: Year-End Boss Fight

| Module | Challenge type | Skill |
|--------|----------------|-------|
| Depreciation | Calculate & journal | Allocate equipment cost over useful life |
| Home office | Percentage method | Business-use portion of rent/utilities |
| Mileage claim | Log + rate | Vehicle deduction vs actual expenses |
| Adjusting entry set | Multi-step boss | All three entry types |
| Accountant handoff | Checklist sim | Export-ready books narrative |

### Boss fight structure

1. **Phase 1** — Depreciation entry for June laptop purchase
2. **Phase 2** — Home office adjustment (% of home expenses)
3. **Phase 3** — Mileage log reconciliation
4. **Phase 4** — Final review: adjusted TB, P&L, BS, handoff checklist

### Badges

- **Depreciation Done**
- **Home Office Pro**
- **Mileage Master**
- **Tax-Ready** — Complete handoff with zero critical errors

### Course completion

Earn **Ledger Quest Graduate** badge; mastery score displayed on profile.

## Content Accuracy Notes

- Use standard double-entry conventions; debits increase assets/expenses, credits increase liabilities/equity/revenue (with explicit teaching moments)
- Sales tax treatment should match a generic jurisdiction model; label as educational simplification where needed
- Depreciation, home office, and mileage examples use clearly stated assumptions (rates, %, useful life) in challenge copy
- All dollar amounts in practice data should reconcile across journal → TB → statements
